Coaching Framework

The INC Coaching Model

The Integrative NLP Coaching model operates at three levels:

Values Level Coaching ‚Äî Purpose, Higher Self, Identity
Area of Life Coaching ‚Äî Values, Beliefs, Breakthrough Session
Situational Coaching (~25-30% of calls) ‚Äî Environment, Perception, Behavior, Strategies, Actions

4 Requisites for Empowerment & Change

  1. Breakthrough Session ‚Äî Releasing baggage
  2. Avatar, Aim & End ‚Äî Goal-setting
  3. Take Purposeful Action
  4. Be 100% Present (SMEP: Spiritual, Mental, Emotional, Physical)

The PAUSE Model (Single-Session Coaching)

  • Pay Attention / Hakalau
  • Active Listening
  • Understand / Appreciate
  • Search for Deeper Issue
  • Execute with Your Language

Avatar ‚Äî BEING Energy ("I am NOUN/NOMINALIZATION")

Your Avatar is your future self ‚Äî who you are meant to be. It covers 10, 5, and 3-year visions and guides your long-term Aim and immediate End Goals.

  • Written in first person, present tense
  • Four paragraphs covering SMEP:
    • Spiritual/Energetic ‚Äî Purpose & Energy
    • Mental ‚Äî Mindset, Beliefs, Thoughts, Focus
    • Emotional ‚Äî How you navigate emotions, EQ
    • Physical ‚Äî Physical body
  • Include your values and keywords
  • Include: aspects achieved (maintain), not yet achieved (manifest), and anything in between
  • Build from: Words ‚Üí Phrases ‚Üí Sentences ‚Üí Paragraphs
  • As you read your Avatar, are you excited?
  • If stuck: Do a mirror visualization and place on timeline

Aim ‚Äî DOING Energy ("I am VERB" / "I am a person who does ACTION")

Your Aim is your direction ‚Äî your "North Star." You may never fully reach it.

  • Written in first person narrative
  • Three paragraphs:
    1. "[Area of Life] is... [definition]" ‚Äî What does this area mean to you? (1 paragraph)
    2. Action statements ‚Äî "What do I need to be DOING to become my Avatar?"
    3. "I want this because..." ‚Äî 1-2 paragraphs on motivation (the WHY)

Check for Values

Your top 4-6 values must appear in both your Avatar and Aim. If values are missing, update the Avatar/Aim to include them.

End ‚Äî HAVING Energy

Your End is a tangible target (earning a specific amount, weighing a specific amount, etc.)

  • Written in first person, present tense
  • Must have a specific date
  • Must fulfill the SMART acronym

üí° CM Advice

Screen share Google Docs while teaching Avatar/Aim/End so clients can see it being built in real-time.

  • Specific ‚Äî Simple
  • Measurable ‚Äî Meaningful to you
  • As if now ‚Äî Write from the perspective of goal achievement date
  • Achievable ‚Äî All Areas of Life
  • Realistic ‚Äî Responsible (Ecological)
  • Timed ‚Äî Toward what you want

The SMART Goal Process (12 Steps)

  1. Identify the Goal
  2. Get the last step
  3. Make IR VAK (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic)
  4. Step into IR (Associate)
  5. Adjust SMDs for the most real/positive feeling
  6. Step out of IR (Dissociate)
  7. Take IR & float above NOW
  8. Energize IR with 4 HA breaths
  9. Take IR & float into FUTURE
  10. Insert IR into FUTURE timeline
  11. Notice how events between NOW and FUTURE re-evaluate themselves
  12. Float back to NOW

Key Reminders

  • Make sure the goal is achievable and ecological ‚Äî if not, your UCM will thwart your efforts (survival brain views failure as a threat)
  • Write in present tense ‚Äî don't confuse your CM by wording things as already done; don't push goals further out with future tense
  • CM = Goal Setter (SMART Goals activate this)
  • UCM = Goal Getter (Clear any baggage preventing goal achievement)

A three-part decision framework used to test whether a goal is right for the client:

‚úÖ YES #1 ‚Äî Higher Self / Values / Intuition

A "yes" means moral compass and goals are aligned. A "no" means a Values conflict.

‚úÖ YES #2 ‚Äî Mental / Conscious Mind / Reason

A "yes" means the ecology works and it makes sense. A "no" means a logical or ecological conflict.

üö´ HELL NO ‚Äî Emotional / Unconscious Mind / Survival/Fear

A "yes" means the goal is too small. A "hell no" means there is something to overcome ‚Äî baggage, or the goal is just big enough to scare them (root chakra pucker).

Key Insight: If there's no "hell no," the client doesn't need a coach to achieve it. The "hell no" gives the opportunity for breakthrough!

The 4-Mat system is a structured approach to teaching and explaining concepts (covered in detail in the Master Practitioner training, Segment 21:1). When explaining any concept to a client, address these four learning styles in order:

  1. WHY ‚Äî Why is this important? Why should the client care? (Feeling learners)
  2. WHAT ‚Äî What is it exactly? Give the content and data. (Thinking learners)
  3. HOW ‚Äî How does it work? Demonstrate and practice. (Doing learners)
  4. WHAT IF ‚Äî What if we applied this? What are the possibilities? (Creative learners)

This ensures your communication reaches all four types of learners during coaching sessions and mentor calls.

Finding the pattern is a combination of Active Listening, Hierarchy of Ideas, Milton Model, and Meta Model ‚Äî plus the art of coaching.

The Process

  1. Chunk Down to go HD on the story
  2. Chunk Up to find the pattern
  3. Chunk Down into the pattern itself
  4. Chunk Laterally to find temporal and context examples of the pattern

Getting the Pattern (Summary)

  1. Chunk down into the story, chunk up to see the pattern
  2. Chunk down into the pattern and lateral chunk to confirm
  3. Ask: "What baggage/pattern would I need to make this my story?"
  4. Confirm the pattern with client
  5. Go through the 4 requisites for change

Key insight: Whatever a client repeatedly brings up is a fractal of a deeper issue. NLP is sometimes known as a pattern interrupt.

An Integrative NLP Coach is NOT:

  • A friend, fixer, healer, guru, gossip, savior, rescuer, crutch, victim, enabler, babysitter, dumping ground, or authority figure
  • Someone who has all the answers or runs their own personal agenda
  • Someone looking to clients for validation or thinking they're better than clients

Avoid These Listening Pitfalls

  • Your Own Needs: Hunger, thirst, headache, other to-dos
  • Judgments & Criticisms: About the client, yourself, or your performance
  • Worries: "Am I doing it right?" "Does my client like me?"
  • Daydreaming
  • The Story: Relating client's story to your own, creating narratives about the client, reminiscing
  • Creating "What Ifs" in your own life based on your client's story
  • Parroting: Mindless repetition (use reflective listening instead)

Other Important Reminders

  • Always ask permission before any process (even if client is venting, they need to consent)
  • A client venting doesn't equal permission to do Ho'oponopono
  • Make sure your client actually needs a coach ‚Äî if they're too invested in their baggage, they may need a therapist
  • If potential client is seeing a therapist, get a referral. Avoid people on certain psych medications.
  • Show Up vs Show Off ‚Äî Get clients results and they'll think you're a badass; badass is a byproduct
  • Beware of leaning too much on AI ‚Äî it could handicap your critical thinking

Mentor Guide

Group Call Structure

Each Mentor Call follows this structure (targeting 60-75 minutes):

  1. Grounding (Call #1: CM leads. Calls #2-6: Mentee volunteer leads)
  2. Exercises ‚Äî 8 minutes per coach + 2 minutes CM feedback (10 min each, or 10+2 for groups of 4)
  3. Repeat until everyone has gone
  4. Q&A: What questions do you have? What did you learn? What do I need to know?
  5. Wrap-Up: Review next call's topics

Exercise Structure

Clients rotate: the Client for one exercise becomes the Coach on the next. Last Coach coaches the first person.

DPH Exercise (Coaches A, B, E)

Script: "Why are you here? Why else? Why else?" ‚Äî Elicit all reasons (target 8-12 items).

Purpose: Get comfortable eliciting reasons for BTS in this area of life, identifying pain points, baggage, negative emotions, limiting beliefs.

Client instruction: Pretend coaching on Career. Roleplay as yourself pre-BTS or any cooperative made-up client.

CM Listens For: Chunking skills, questioning skills, picking up on tone and what's not said.

Values Exercise (Coaches C, D)

Script A: Explain what values are, why they're important, define complex equivalence. What does it mean to have a conflict within a single value? Between two values?

Script B: Move a Value. Use the last 3 Career Values to move the last value up by 1 in priority.

Client instruction: Be curious. Ask clarifying questions a non-NLP person would ask. If Coach uses NLP jargon, ask them to define it.

CM Target Definition: "Values determine how you spend your time and how you evaluate the time spent."

Coaching Preparation Checklist

Before every coaching session (15-30 minutes):

  1. Yourself and Your Space: Clear environment, minimize distractions
  2. Review Client Notes: Refresh on last session, tasks assigned, progress
  3. Ground Yourself: Grounded, Clear, Calm, Focused, Present, Flexible
  4. Send 2 Aka Connections & Bring Mana Up
  5. Set Your Focus and Intention: What is your intention for this session?
  6. Hold the Client's Highest Vision
  1. Pitch Test / Establish Rapport ‚Äî Match & Mirror: physiology, tone, rep systems, breathing, key words, common interests
  2. Ask Questions ‚Äî "For what purpose‚Ä¶?" Discover IR, desired state, MOW, strategies and values. Frame the why.
  3. Find Pain/Want & Establish Value ‚Äî Conditional close: "Do you see any value in this‚Ä¶?" Use tag questions. Find the hook.
  4. Link the Want/Value to Your Service ‚Äî "Is it fair enough‚Ä¶" "What would happen if‚Ä¶" "Compared to‚Ä¶" (Contrast Frame). Use their language.
  5. Close ‚Äî Ask for the order, keep quiet. If no: ignore first time. If no again: act surprised, handle objections, go back to #3. If yes: future pace satisfaction, get referrals.

Only Four Objections

  • Not enough time ‚Üí One hour a week
  • Not enough money ‚Üí Contrast reframe (cost of pain vs cost of coaching)
  • I don't believe you ‚Üí Testimonials, success rate, guarantee
  • It won't work for me ‚Üí Guarantee, research, "That's exactly why you should come see me!"

Elevator Pitch Template

(Most/A lot) [Ideal Client] (think/feel/believe) [PAIN], when (it's/they're) actually [A-HA]. I help [Ideal Client] [solution/benefit] so that [pain/~pain] is gone.

Rules: 5-25 second attention span, 35 words or less, connect to pain, describe benefits, chunk up, limit jargon, K.I.S.S.
