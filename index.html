In-Program Coaching ‚Äî Course Overview & Requirements

Course Overview

It is time to put the skills you learned in the Coaching Skills module and INC Live to the test. In this three-month program, you will continue to be paired with your In-Program Coach and an In-Program Client for weekly coaching sessions. You will also be coaching a minimum of one Pro Bono Client that you are responsible for obtaining; we recommend procuring two Pro Bono Clients.

You will draw from NLP tools and techniques, situational and area of life coaching, and feedback from your Coach Mentor to take your coaching to the next level in this In-Program Coaching module.

Online Course Format:

  • 3-Month Online Learning Course
  • 3 Webinars (not 12 ‚Äî note correction from some docs)
  • 6 Mentor Group Calls
  • Tuesdays at 5:30pm Pacific, 60-90 minutes, via Zoom
  • NOTE: Dates/Times may vary

Grading: Credit / No Credit

In order to receive Credit, you must meet ALL of the following course requirements:

  • Attend monthly Empowerment webinars (1 per month, 3 total in this module)
  • Attend a total of 6 Mentor Calls with your assigned Coach Mentor (approximately 60-90 minutes every other week); actively participate with camera on during full duration
  • Complete BTS in the area of Career and 12 weeks of coaching with your In-Program Coach
  • Complete BTS in the area of Career and 12 weeks of coaching with your In-Program Client
  • Complete BTS (area of life TBD) and 11 weeks of coaching with at least one Pro Bono "practice" Client; we recommend two
  • Coaching reflection form to be completed and submitted every 3 weeks
  • Schedule and complete a total of 5 Observed Coaching Calls with your Pro Bono Client, observed by your Coach Mentor (Observed Calls must be in English)
  • Submit 3 final forms that ask for feedback on your experience with your In-Program Coach, In-Program Client, and your Coach Mentor
  • Pass the final Oral Exam

üëÅÔ∏è Observed Calls

5 observed calls with your Pro Bono Client, November‚ÄìJanuary

Your Coach Mentor (CM) needs to observe a total of 5 calls with your Pro Bono Client, November‚ÄìJanuary. As soon as you get your schedule set up with your Pro Bono client, please contact your CM with the dates and times, so you can schedule your monthly observed call for November.

If you are able to map out all 11 Pro Bono coaching sessions, send them all to your CM so you can get December and January on your CM's calendar. Make sure the January observed call is after the 6th group call.

Pre-frame Observed Calls ASAP with your primary Pro Bono Client. Let them know:
  • The purpose is for your CM to give you feedback and help you hone your coaching skill sets
  • Your CM will listen on mute
  • Anything on the Observed Call will be kept confidential with both you and your CM
  • Your CM is only listening and analyzing your coaching, not the content being shared

Logistics:

  • Invite your CM to your call BEFORE your Client joins (whether by phone or Zoom)
  • Let your CM mute themselves and go off camera BEFORE adding your Client to the call
  • Your CM will give feedback immediately after your Client leaves the call
  • Depending on schedules, your CM may request an occasional recorded call to give feedback if they cannot attend live

What Your CM Is Listening For

These are the elements your CM evaluates, with suggested time spans to keep you on track for a ~1 hour call:

ElementTimeDetails

Coach Mentor Advice: Create a takeaway "map" by deliberately collecting positive client takeaways at the end of each call into a single document. If you do this, you can easily use it to create a hypnotic induction for your last call in the coaching cycle that reminds the Client what they have accomplished with you.

üé§ Oral Exam

The final assessment ‚Äî January 14‚Äì20, 2026

All six (6) Group Calls are preparing you for your Oral Exam in January. If you feel confident in everything that you've gone over in the Group Calls, you'll do just fine.

Pro Tip: If time allows, schedule your Oral Exam right after a January Observed Call (after the 6th Group Call), so all of the content and experience is "fresh" on your mind since you'll already be in Coaching Mode and have completed multiple practice runs on the Group Calls.

Format:

  • You will be asked a total of 4 questions that parallel scenarios from the Group Calls
  • Like the Group Calls, the scenario will be read, and you'll be the Coach
  • Your CM will role-play as the Client in the exercise
  • Unlike Group Calls (which are timed), in the Oral Exam you get to keep going until you (as the Coach) feel like you have fully completed the prompt

Pass / No Pass:

  • The Oral Exam is either a "Pass" or "No Pass" exam
  • Your CM will verbally tell you your result at the end of the call
  • After assembling their written feedback, they will email it to you

To Graduate from the Module:

  1. All calls complete
  2. All forms submitted by the final deadline (Jan 27)
  3. "Pass" on your Oral Exam

Timing: No more than 1 hour.

üîÑ Ideal Pro Bono Call Flow

Week-by-week guide for coaching your Pro Bono Client(s)

Coach Mentor Advice: To be sure you graduate from this module, it is recommended to keep notes on two Pro Bono Clients in case one drops out. You are required to coach at least one (1) Pro Bono Client for 12 weeks to graduate ‚Äî there are no exceptions. Stagger their Breakthrough Sessions (BTS) so you only do 1 BTS per week.

Week 1: Breakthrough Session

Complete after the first Group Call:

  • Detailed Personal History
  • 1st set of Values
  • Release work
  • Hierarchy the Values List and have them talk about why each value is important (helps dig up more baggage & associates them)

Finishing touches: 2nd set of Values, Clean-Up, Motivation + Threshold

Week 2: Coaching Call #1 ‚Äî Teach Avatar

This is Coaching Call #1 of 11 for your Pro Bono Client(s).

  • Teach Avatar ("Being" Goal)
  • Help them see their vision of who they want to be in 5‚Äì10 years, depending on how far into the future the client can see

Assign Task: Have Client write out their 5-year or 10-year Avatar

Week 3: Give Feedback on Avatar & Teach Aim

  • Give Feedback on Avatar & make any necessary changes
  • Teach Aim ("Doing" Goal) ‚Äî help them see their big-picture vision

Assign Task: Have Client write out their Aim

Week 4: Put Avatar/Aim in the Future & Teach SMART Goal

  • Put Avatar / Aim in the Future on the same call
  • NOTE: With Avatar & Aim, leave out Step 2 in the process on page 19 in your INC Manual.
  • Teach SMART Goal ("Having" Goal)
    • This goal should be something they achieve within your Pro Bono coaching relationship
    • Teach the S.M.A.R.T. acronym
    • Teach the simple approach to setting a SMART goal with as much detail as needed

Assign Task: Write out their SMART Goal in detail

Week 5: Feedback on SMART Goal & Design Action Plan

  • Give feedback on SMART Goal ‚Äî have Client make any necessary changes
  • Avatar / AIM: If not yet put into the future by Week 5, do that first
    • Order: 1st ‚Äî Avatar + AIM (altogether), 2nd ‚Äî End (SMART) Goal
  • Help them Design Their Action Plan
    • Discuss how to design their action plan to achieve their goal
    • Have them identify the main targets to achieve their goal
    • Hierarchy the list in order of priority

Assign Task: Ask Client to self-assign 1‚Äì3 tasks based on their first target

Weeks 6‚Äì8: Area of Life / Situational Coaching

Focus on situational coaching and working through the client's challenges within their area of life.

Week 9: Discuss Next Goal or Reset Trajectory

  • Celebrate at 90% and set a new goal

Assign Task: Write out the next goal or revise current action plan to meet existing goal

Week 10: Coaching + Feedback Form + Referrals

  • Area of Life / Situational coaching
  • The Pro Bono Client Feedback Form will be sent to your Pro Bono Client
  • Ask for Referrals & Testimonial (do this BEFORE the final call)

Week 11: Coaching + Design a New Goal

  • Area of Life / Situational coaching
  • Design a New Goal ‚Äî should be a 1, 3, or 5 year goal
  • Continue Referral / Testimonial conversation

Week 12: Final Call

  • Put Client's new SMART Goal into the Future
  • Design their new Action Plan
  • Continue Referral conversation
  • 5 Step Sales Process to Re-Sign the Client for another 3 months
If your calls get behind: Double up your calls in a week to get caught up. Please avoid having more than two calls within a 7-day time period.

üéØ Avatar, Aim & End

The three-part goal-setting framework: Being ‚Üí Doing ‚Üí Having

Coach Mentor Advice: Utilize Google Docs or Zoom screen share when you teach Avatar / Aim / End while it's being built. This adds a Visual component while you teach it. It not only allows for feedback and corrections, but also allows for quick and easy editing, where you can copy and paste material into the proper category. This also puts the client at ease for reorganization later.

AVATAR ‚Äî "Being" Energy üåü

Written in first-person narrative form, present tense, 4 paragraphs (SMEP):

ParagraphFocusDescription
1Spiritual / EnergeticPurpose, energy, connection
2MentalMindset, beliefs, thoughts, focus
3EmotionalEQ, navigating emotions, cultivating positive emotions
4PhysicalPhysical body, health, vitality

Key Points:

  • Use "I am NOUN / NOMINALIZATION" statements for BEING energy
  • If the Client gets stuck, guide them to visualize seeing their future self (mirror visualization), then have them describe that. Utilize tools & language patterns you've learned to encourage thought flow.
  • Avatar includes things you are already being and things you have not yet become
  • A person only has ONE Avatar. The Avatar is a living, breathing document and can be changed.
  • Place on timeline after final draft
  • Include your values and any keywords
  • As you read your Avatar, are you excited?

Things to include: Aspects you've already achieved and want to maintain ¬∑ Aspects you have not yet achieved but want to manifest ¬∑ Anything in between. Build from: Words ‚Üí Phrases ‚Üí Sentences ‚Üí Paragraphs.

From Live Training Notes ‚Äî "Hell No" after BTS: The goals you set should instigate a bit of a root chakra pucker, knowing that they need to level up in order to be this.

AIM ‚Äî "Doing" Energy üèπ

Written in first-person narrative form, 3 paragraphs:

ParagraphStructureDescription
1"[Area of Life] is‚Ä¶ [definition]"Define the area of life and what it means to you
2"I am VERB" or "I am a person who does ACTION"DOING energy statements
3"I want this because‚Ä¶"Your motivation and "why"

Key Points:

  • Use "I am VERB" or "I am a person who does ACTION" statements for DOING energy
  • These paragraphs mirror what Dr. Matt has his clients do
  • Aim is a direction ‚Äî your "North Star." You may never reach it.
  • Looking at your Avatar, ask: "What is my direction?" "What do I need to be doing to become that Avatar?"

‚úÖ Check for Values (Between Avatar and Aim)

Between the Avatar and Aim, make sure that at least the top 4‚Äì6 values are represented ‚Äî either with the label they use for their values or words that represent the complex equivalence of any unnamed values.

END Goal ‚Äî "Having" Energy üèÜ

(TIP: Review Keys to an Achievable Outcome in INC Manual)

  • Written in first-person narrative form, present tense
  • HAVING energy
  • Fulfills SMART Goal criteria
  • Written from the perspective of a specific date in which it is fulfilled ‚Äî think of it as a journal entry from the future where everything has gone well in this area of life
  • End is a tangible target (earning a specific amount, weighing a specific amount)

Aim vs. End ‚Äî Key Distinction

Aim üèπ

Aim is a direction. Your "North Star." You may never reach it. It's about what you need to be DOING to become your Avatar.

End üèÜ

End is a tangible target. A specific, measurable outcome with a date. It fulfills the SMART criteria.

  1. 1st ‚Äî Avatar + AIM (altogether)
  2. 2nd ‚Äî End (SMART) Goal

SMART Goal Process (12 Steps)

  1. Identify the Goal
  2. Get the last step
  3. Make IR VAK (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic)
  4. Step into IR (Associate)
  5. Adjust SMDs for the most real/positive feeling
  6. Step out of IR (Dissociate)
  7. Take IR & float above NOW
  8. Energize IR with 4 HA breaths
  9. Take IR & float into FUTURE
  10. Insert IR into FUTURE timeline

‚ö° Common Pitfalls

Challenges to watch for ‚Äî and how to handle them

Pitfall #1: Identifying the Right Area of Life

Big Picture:

  • What area of life to do a BTS on is determined by your Client's goal
  • Look for the area of life in which achieving a goal would make maximum impact. You're finding the root of the presenting problem to match up with the area of life.
  • When in doubt, ask your client what singular goal they would need to laser-focus on and achieve to have the greatest impact in their lives, and reverse engineer the area of life from that.
Area of Life to AVOID (if it's their very 1st BTS): "Personal Growth & Development"

"Personal Growth & Development" may be appropriate after 2‚Äì3 breakthrough sessions in other areas. Most Clients who say they want PG&D actually need something else. All aspects of Integrative NLP Coaching contribute to personal growth, and starting with "Personal Growth" first often dilutes precision.

Examples:

Example: Client unhappy ‚Äî job and relationships suffering ‚ñº

Client tells you they are unhappy because their job and relationships are suffering. With further questioning, they reveal drinking too much and overeating, becoming overweight, causing problems in job and relationships.

‚Üí BTS should be on Physical Health because the issue is directly correlated to their body, and their goal is to lose weight and improve their physical body.

NOTE: If their Greater Problem revolves around how they perceive/feel about their body, then that's an appropriate BTS in PG&D, which is very rare for a first BTS.

Example: Client struggling with dating ‚ñº

Client is struggling with dating due to lack of confidence and lack of time from working too much. You find out they have no choice but to be a workaholic because they need more financial resources.

‚Üí BTS should be on Career because they need to make more money.

Pitfall #2: Challenges with In-Program Coach or Client

Coach Mentors observe Pro-Bono calls (not In-Program calls) to help you in a setting where the client is 100% new to it all.

Avoid coaching your Coach!

You are the Client, and you need to remain the Client. If you're about to say something fueled by your knowledge of NLP (beyond communication), refrain from saying it.

What you CAN do:

  • Tell them you have a part you'd like to integrate
  • Tell them a re-labeled Value doesn't resonate and you'd prefer a different term
  • Advocate for what you need if you're not getting it
  • If that doesn't work, report repeated issues to your Coach Mentor

If your In-Program Client is coaching you:

  • Reiterate the boundaries of the coaching relationship
  • If it continues, report to your CM ‚Äî policies are in place to deal with this
"Kill the dragon while it's a baby." The "dragon" is the problem or issue, not your In-Program Client. Whether it's a negative emotion or a conflict, take care of it as soon as you see it because it will only get bigger.

Pitfall #3: The Tough Client ‚Äî "The Stone Sharpens the Sword"

Tough clients or tough situations will make you a better coach. Maybe your Client is doubting that change can happen so fast, or they forgot their breakthroughs and lose focus.

This is a great opportunity to review the Presuppositions of NLP and Prime Directives of the unconscious mind. NLP is a "Do With" process, not a "Do To" process ‚Äî you need unconscious permission for Change Work.

Key Distinctions:

  • Baggage and Patterns are two different things. Sometimes the Client may still be running old patterns and mistaking them for baggage.
  • "All learning, behavior, and change is unconscious" ‚Äî this is where takeaways come into play, consciously reminding the Client of unconscious changes.

Helpful Endings for a Coaching Cycle:

  • An induction to recap your coaching time together
  • A visualization exercise
  • Re-iterate takeaways you've compiled to remind them how far they've come

Remember the 4 Agreements:

  1. "Be impeccable with your word" ‚Äî speaking with love, truth, and integrity
  2. "Don't take anything personally" ‚Äî what others do is a projection of their reality
  3. "Don't make assumptions" ‚Äî ask questions and clarify doubts
  4. "Always do your best" ‚Äî know that you do your best at the time you're doing it
Pro Bono clients are a perfect opportunity to learn and grow, and that's what this season in your coaching evolution is all about.
